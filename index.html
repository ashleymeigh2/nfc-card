Introduction

Hi! I'm Ashley Meigh, a Software Scientist at the ISIS Neutron and Muon Source, where I develop software for neutron imaging and tomography. My work focuses on creating tools that allow researchers to reconstruct, analyse and visualise neutron imaging data, helping scientists investigate the internal structure of materials using neutrons.

Away from work I have far too many hobbies. I currently cultivate 22 different mushroom species, photograph fungi across the UK, maintain a digital fungal archive, volunteer on local Toad Patrols, experiment with biological computing, build mushroom-controlled robots, knit neutron imaging data into textiles and occasionally remember to water my houseplants.

Most of my projects start with a question that probably shouldn't be asked, such as:

  • 🍄 Can mushrooms control a robot?
  • 🧶 Can neutron diffraction data become a jumper?
  • 📱 Can fungi be collected like Pokémon?
  • 🐸 How many toads can be safely escorted across a road?

This website is a collection of those projects, where science, engineering and creativity occasionally produce something useful... and sometimes just something interesting.
